About us

A shelf that started with one box.

Booklane RYK began as a single wooden book box outside a tuition centre in Rahim Yar Khan. It's now a small, stubborn network of volunteers who believe a book within walking distance changes what a neighbourhood reads.

HOW IT STARTED

From one box to a lane.

A handful of students and teachers noticed the same thing: there was nowhere nearby to simply pick up a book, read it, and pass it on. Public libraries were far, expensive, or unwelcoming to younger readers. So we built a box, filled it with donated books, and left it on a street corner.

Within a month the box was empty and full again, several times over. That's when "one box" became "a lane" — a small trail of book stops that people could walk between.

WHO WE ARE

Run by readers, not an office.

We have no headquarters and no paid staff. Booklane RYK is carried by university students, school teachers, shopkeepers who host a box outside their shutter, and parents who translate storybooks in their spare time.

Decisions are made at monthly meet-ups, usually at whichever book lane has the most shade that week.

What keeps us going

Three things we've learned.

01

Proximity beats promotion

A book five minutes away gets read more than a library announcement. We chase distance, not attention.

02

Writers need readers, not just grades

A student essay marked only by a teacher disappears into a drawer. Publishing it, even locally, changes how seriously a young writer takes their own voice.

03

Small and local is repairable

A box can be rebuilt in an afternoon. We'd rather run fourteen small lanes we can fix ourselves than one large library we depend on others for.

Come find us

Every lane is open to anyone who wants to read, write, or build one with us.
